1. Introduction

The TSSA G3 Propane Gas Technician certification is your entry point into the propane gas industry in Ontario. This license qualifies you to work on domestic propane appliances up to 45,000 BTU/hr input, including common household equipment like stoves, water heaters, and fireplaces.

Passing the G3 exam requires thorough preparation, understanding of the CSA B149.2 Propane Installation Code, and practical knowledge of propane systems. This guide provides you with proven strategies to prepare effectively and pass your exam with confidence.

Who This Guide Is For

This guide is designed for aspiring propane gas technicians preparing for the TSSA G3 certification exam. Whether you're just starting your journey or looking to enhance your preparation, you'll find valuable insights and resources here.

2. G3 License Overview

What is a G3 License?

The G3 license is issued by the Technical Standards and Safety Authority (TSSA) and allows technicians to install, alter, maintain, and service domestic propane gas appliances with an input rating not exceeding 45,000 BTU/hr.

Scope of Work

With a G3 license, you can work on:

  • Domestic propane ranges and ovens
  • Propane water heaters (up to 45,000 BTU/hr)
  • Propane fireplaces and space heaters
  • Propane dryers
  • Small propane appliances in residential settings

Career Path

The G3 license is typically the first step in a propane gas technician career. After gaining two years of experience with a G3 license, you can advance to the G2 certification, which covers natural gas systems and larger appliances.

3. Exam Format and Structure

Exam Specifications

Aspect Details
Number of Questions 165 multiple choice questions
Time Limit 3.5 hours
Passing Score 75% (124 correct answers required)
Format In-person written exam at approved TSSA testing centers
Reference Materials CSA B149.1-25 and CSA B149.2-25 Code books provided during exam

Key Topic Areas

The exam covers the following main areas:

  • CSA Units 1-9 of the Gas Technician Learning Materials
  • CSA B149.1-25 Natural Gas Installation Code
  • CSA B149.2-25 Propane Installation Code
  • Gas Appliance Fundamentals
  • Piping Systems and Installation
  • Safety Standards and Regulations
  • Combustion Principles
  • Ventilation Requirements

Time Management

With 3.5 hours for 165 questions, you have approximately 1.3 minutes per question. This moves quickly, so practice working at this pace during your study sessions.

4. Essential Study Resources

Primary Resources

The most important study materials for the G3 exam include:

CSA B149.1-25 Natural Gas Installation Code

The B149.1 code is one of the two essential code books for your exam. It covers all aspects of natural gas installation, including appliance requirements, piping systems, and safety standards.

CSA B149.2-25 Propane Installation Code

The B149.2 code is equally important and covers all aspects of propane (LPG) installation, from appliance selection to piping requirements and propane-specific safety considerations. You must become thoroughly familiar with both code books.

CSA Gas Technician Learning Materials (Units 1-9)

These comprehensive learning modules cover fundamental gas technology concepts, safety procedures, and technical knowledge required for the G3 certification. All nine units are testable on the exam.

TSSA Regulations

Understanding the Technical Standards and Safety Act and Ontario Regulations is crucial. These documents outline the legal requirements and authority under which you'll operate.

5. Effective Study Strategies

Create a Study Schedule

Dedicate 8-12 weeks of focused study time before your exam. With 165 questions covering extensive material, thorough preparation is essential.

Recommended Study Timeline

Weeks 1-3: Study CSA Gas Technician Learning Materials (Units 1-9), taking detailed notes on key concepts

Weeks 4-6: Read through both CSA B149.1 and B149.2 codes completely, familiarizing yourself with structure and terminology

Weeks 7-9: Focus on critical code sections, start practice questions, identify weak areas

Weeks 10-11: Intensive practice exams, review challenging topics, practice code lookups

Week 12: Final review, simulate full exam conditions (3.5 hours), review critical code sections

Master Both CSA Code Books

Both the B149.1 (Natural Gas) and B149.2 (Propane) codes are essential resources. Here's how to study them effectively:

  • Read both codes cover to cover - Don't skip sections that seem unimportant
  • Understand the structure of each - Know how to quickly locate information during the exam
  • Compare and contrast - Note differences between natural gas and propane requirements
  • Focus on Tables and Figures - Many questions reference specific charts and diagrams
  • Highlight key requirements - Mark critical measurements, clearances, and specifications
  • Practice code lookups - Time yourself finding specific code sections in both books

Active Learning Techniques

Engage with the material actively rather than passively reading:

  1. Practice Questions: Complete hundreds of practice questions to reinforce your knowledge
  2. Create Flashcards: Make cards for key terms, definitions, and code requirements
  3. Teach Someone Else: Explaining concepts to others reinforces your understanding
  4. Hands-on Practice: If possible, observe or assist with real propane installations
  5. Join Study Groups: Connect with other G3 candidates to share insights and quiz each other

Focus Areas

Pay special attention to these high-yield topics:

  • All CSA Gas Technician Learning Materials (Units 1-9)
  • Appliance clearances and spacing requirements (both natural gas and propane)
  • Piping materials, sizing, and installation methods
  • Ventilation requirements for gas appliances
  • Combustion air requirements and calculations
  • Gas pressure testing procedures and safety checks
  • Leak detection and emergency procedures
  • Propane storage, handling, and delivery regulations
  • Connection types, flexible connectors, and fittings
  • Differences between natural gas and propane installations
  • Appliance conversion procedures

The 80/20 Rule

Approximately 80% of exam questions come from 20% of the material. Focus your study time on the core topics that appear most frequently in practice exams.

6. Exam Day Tips

Before the Exam

  • Arrive Early: Plan to arrive at least 30 minutes before your scheduled time
  • Bring Required ID: Government-issued photo ID is mandatory
  • Get Good Rest: A well-rested mind performs significantly better
  • Eat Properly: Have a nutritious meal before the exam to maintain energy
  • Review Basics: Quickly review key formulas and tables the morning of the exam

During the Exam

  1. Read Questions Carefully: Don't rush - many wrong answers come from misreading questions
  2. Use Both Code Books Efficiently: Know how to navigate both B149.1 and B149.2 codes quickly
  3. Flag Difficult Questions: Mark questions you're unsure about and return to them later
  4. Manage Your Time: With 165 questions in 3.5 hours, keep moving and don't spend too long on any single question
  5. Eliminate Wrong Answers: If you're unsure, eliminate obviously incorrect options first
  6. Check Your Work: If time permits, review your answers before submitting

Code Book Navigation Tips

Both CSA B149.1-25 and B149.2-25 code books will be provided during the exam. Master these navigation skills for both books:

  • Use the Table of Contents to quickly locate major sections in each code
  • Familiarize yourself with the index for specific terms in both books
  • Know where key tables are located (pipe sizing, clearances, etc.) in each code
  • Understand the numbering system to find subsections quickly
  • Practice looking up specific requirements in under 30 seconds
  • Learn to quickly identify whether a question requires B149.1 (natural gas) or B149.2 (propane)

Test-Taking Strategy

Make two passes through the exam: First, answer all questions you're confident about. Second, tackle the more challenging questions with the remaining time. This ensures you capture all the "easy" points.

7. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Study Phase Mistakes

  • Cramming Last Minute: Start studying early and spread your learning over weeks
  • Ignoring Practice Exams: Practice questions are crucial for identifying weak areas
  • Only Reading the Code: You must actively engage with the material through practice
  • Skipping "Easy" Sections: Every section of the code can appear on the exam
  • Not Timing Practice Sessions: Practice under real exam time constraints

Exam Day Mistakes

  • Rushing Through Questions: Take time to read each question fully
  • Second-Guessing Correct Answers: Your first instinct is often right
  • Leaving Questions Blank: Always make your best guess - there's no penalty for wrong answers
  • Poor Time Management: Don't spend 10 minutes on one difficult question
  • Not Using the Code Books: When in doubt, look it up in the provided B149.1 or B149.2 codes

Learn from Others' Experiences

Many candidates report that they knew the material but lost points due to test anxiety or poor time management. Practice exam simulations help you build confidence and pacing skills.

8. After the Exam

Exam Results

After completing the in-person written exam, you'll receive your results according to TSSA's processing timeline. If you pass, congratulations! If not, don't be discouraged - many successful technicians needed multiple attempts.

If You Pass

  1. Apply for Your License: Complete the TSSA license application process
  2. Find Employment: Start gaining practical experience with your G3 license
  3. Continue Learning: Stay updated with code changes and best practices
  4. Plan for G2: After 2 years, consider advancing to G2 certification for natural gas systems

If You Don't Pass

Don't give up! Many successful technicians required multiple exam attempts. Here's what to do:

  • Review Your Results: Identify which sections you struggled with
  • Wait Period: There's typically a mandatory waiting period before retaking
  • Targeted Study: Focus your restudy efforts on weak areas
  • More Practice Questions: Complete additional practice exams in problem areas
  • Consider Training: Look into formal training courses if self-study isn't working

Advancing Your Career

The G3 license is just the beginning. Here's how to continue growing:

  • Gain hands-on experience with various propane systems and appliances
  • Learn from experienced technicians and mentors
  • Stay current with CSA code updates and industry changes
  • Plan to obtain your G2 license after meeting the experience requirements
  • Consider specializations like commercial propane systems or training roles

Career Pathway

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long should I study for the G3 exam?

A: Most candidates need 8-12 weeks of consistent study (15-20 hours per week) to adequately prepare for the comprehensive 165-question exam. Adjust based on your background knowledge and learning pace.

Q: Is the G3 exam harder than G2?

A: The G3 exam is actually quite comprehensive with 165 questions covering both natural gas and propane codes plus all nine CSA Gas Technician Learning units. While it covers domestic appliances (‚â§45,000 BTU/hr), the breadth of material requires thorough preparation.

Q: Can I use the code books during the exam?

A: Yes! Both the CSA B149.1-25 (Natural Gas) and CSA B149.2-25 (Propane) Installation Codes are provided during the exam. However, you must know how to navigate both books quickly - don't rely on looking up every answer.

Q: What's the pass rate for the G3 exam?

A: TSSA doesn't publish official pass rates, but most training programs report rates between 60-80%. Proper preparation significantly improves your chances.

Q: Do I need practical experience before taking the exam?

A: No practical experience is required to write the G3 exam. However, hands-on experience greatly helps with understanding the concepts and will be necessary after you get your license.

Q: How much does the G3 exam cost?

A: As of 2026, the G3 exam fee is approximately $200-$250. Check the TSSA website for current pricing.

Q: What if I fail the exam?

A: You can retake the exam after a waiting period (typically 30 days). Use this time to review weak areas and complete more practice questions. Many successful technicians needed multiple attempts.

Q: How long is the G3 license valid?

A: G3 licenses must be renewed every 5 years. You'll need to demonstrate continued competency and pay renewal fees.

Q: Can I work independently with just a G3 license?

A: Yes, but only within the scope of the G3 license (domestic propane appliances ‚â§45,000 BTU/hr). You may also need business licenses depending on your location.

Q: What's the difference between B149.1 and B149.2?

A: B149.1 covers natural gas installations, while B149.2 covers propane (LPG) installations. The G3 exam tests both codes equally, as gas technicians need to understand both natural gas and propane systems, even though the G3 license limits you to domestic propane appliances ‚â§45,000 BTU/hr.
